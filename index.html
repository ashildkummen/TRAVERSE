Up to a fifth of hospital patients with Covid-19 contracted the disease while being treated in hospital for another illness.

TRAVERSE proposes a new technology to keep staff and patients safe, saving lives, and rebuilding public trust in healthcare environments.

What is TRAVERSE?

Fear of Covid-19 has led patients to decline critical treatment, in the effort to avoid hospital settings that they no longer deem safe2. Even as the number of Covid-19 cases decline in many places, patients with cancer, heart disease and strokes, among others, are delaying or forgoing critical procedures that could keep them alive.

We propose implementing a real-time tracking system which will monitor staff and visitor mobility in the hospital setting. Sensors are placed in each hospital zone so that data is collected when and where a person is as they move through the hospital. Using this spatio-temporal data, we perform computational analysis to make causal inferences about which events were more likely to have caused a covid-19 positive case.

With this information we can prioritise hospital management and resources where it is most needed, mitigating the spread of the virus and protecting staff and patients.

Who are we?

TRAVERSE is an idea developed by three UCL graduates during the Covid-19 Competition. We all have a passion for using technology to improve upon healthcare. Our backgrounds are Medical Sciences, Psychology, and Computer Science.
